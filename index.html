Elizabeth Fons

AllianceBernstein, London.
School of Computer Science, University of Manchester.

I’m a Marie Curie Fellow currently doing a PhD in Computer Science at the University of Manchester, and a Research Associate at AllianceBernstein, London. The broad topic of my research is the study of machine learning applications for systematic investing, covering hidden Markov models, unsupervised feature selection and deep reinforcement learning. My work is funded by the European Commission as part of the BigDataFinance project.

I hold an MSc in physics from University of Buenos Aires, and I completed my MSc thesis in the ATLAS calorimetry group at the Max Planck Institute for Physics in Munich. My project dealt with improving the event selection of W-bosons during collisions inside the ATLAS detector.
