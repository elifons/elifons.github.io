I'm a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marie_Sk%C5%82odowska-Curie_Actions">Marie Curie Fellow</a> currently doing a PhD in Computer Science at the <a href="https://www.manchester.ac.uk/">University of Manchester</a>, and a Research Associate at <a href="https://www.alliancebernstein.com">AllianceBernstein, London</a>. The broad topic of my research is the study of machine learning applications for systematic investing, covering hidden Markov models, unsupervised feature selection and deep reinforcement learning. My work is funded by the <i>European Commission</i> as part of the
<a href="https://bigdatafinance.eu/">
BigDataFinance
</a>
project.

I hold an MSc in physics from <i>University of Buenos Aires</i>, and I completed my MSc thesis in the 
<a href="https://www.mpp.mpg.de/en/research/structure-of-matter/atlas-detector/atlas-calorimetry/">ATLAS calorimetry group</a>
at the
<a href="https://www.mpp.mpg.de/en/">Max Planck Institute for Physics</a> in Munich. My project dealt with improving the event selection of W-bosons during collisions inside the ATLAS detector.
